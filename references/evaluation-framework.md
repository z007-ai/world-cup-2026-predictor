# Evaluation Framework

## Scoring Rubric

Use a lightweight 1-10 score for each serious contender. Exact numbers are optional, but the dimensions should stay consistent.

1. Team quality
   Overall level of the starting XI and the likely best tournament lineup.
2. Star power
   Ability of elite players to decide knockout matches.
3. Depth
   Quality of rotation options, bench game-changers, and injury resilience.
4. Balance
   Whether the side is structurally sound across attack, midfield, defense, and set pieces.
5. Coaching
   Tactical clarity, in-game adjustments, and tournament pragmatism.
6. Form
   Recent performance in competitive matches, not just friendlies.
7. Tournament temperament
   Experience, composure, and track record in high-pressure fixtures.
8. Path
   Expected difficulty of group and knockout route.
9. Risk
   Injury exposure, age concentration, defensive fragility, finishing variance, or overreliance on one star.

## Interpreting Scores

- `8.5+` means true title favorite territory.
- `7.5-8.4` means plausible champion with a favorable path.
- `6.5-7.4` means dark horse or quarterfinal-level side.
- Below `6.5` usually means outsider unless the bracket opens up dramatically.

Do not average mechanically if one risk is catastrophic. A flawed defense or shallow squad can matter more than a high average.

## Tie-Break Logic

If two teams look close, break ties in this order:

1. Better knockout-proof defense
2. Better midfield control and tactical flexibility
3. More players who can decide a tight match
4. Easier likely path
5. Lower injury and availability risk

## Forecast Types

### Champion Forecast

Output:

- predicted champion
- runner-up or strongest alternative
- 2-4 sentence justification
- confidence level

### Contender Ranking

Output a ranked top 5 or top 8 with one sentence per team.

### Dark Horse Forecast

A dark horse should be strong enough to plausibly reach the semifinal but not already be an obvious top favorite.

Explain:

- what makes the team dangerous
- what ceiling they have
- what likely stops them

### Matchup Forecast

For a single matchup:

- estimate win probabilities if useful
- explain tactical matchup
- state the biggest swing factor

## Style Guidance

- Prefer crisp arguments over fan-style rhetoric.
- Avoid long historical detours unless they affect the current squad.
- Use cautious language for uncertain roster assumptions.
- Name the date of any rankings, form sample, or injury update you rely on.
